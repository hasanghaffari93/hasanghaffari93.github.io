I contributed to a project that has been implemented in RoboKids, an interactive robotic play center. Here's how it works: Children can color various drawings and place their painitng into a designated box. The system uses advanced image processing to recognize and extract different elements from the painitng. Once the painitng is processed, it is animated and displayed on a large screen. For example, if a child colors a sun, the sun is brought to life on the screen, creating an effect where it appears to move and transition into daylight.

My role in this project focused on the image processing aspects. I developed and implemented the algorithms for recognizing and interpreting the drawings, ensuring that the animated representations on the screen accurately reflected the children's artwork in real-time.
